Meet Job Match

Job Match is a job search website that aims to connect job seekers and employers in a similar manner to Tinder. Caracas Coffee Logo

Finished logo design two colours on a mobile app

The Breif

Background:

Caracas Coffee is a gourmet coffee company that imports and roasts its own range of coffees. They also have two city centre cafés. They would like to be considered a gourmet coffee roaster which appeals to the discerning coffee drinkers. To that end they would like to be thought of as a counterpoint to the high street chain coffee store brands.

Customers:

Their customers would consider themselves to be discriminating coffee drinkers who will purposely seek out a quality coffee either in the café or purchase their ground coffee in retail outlets.

Design:

The name evokes Latin America and that is very much their intention – they would like to convey an earthy and honest feel as their coffee comes directly from the growers. As the coffee is also roasted in Ireland there is a local take they might like to include in the branding elements.

‘Blossom’ is an annual garden show that showcases the best in flowers, plants, boutique gardens and gourmet food. It is an outdoor family event which takes place in a city centre park on August 14th.

The website is being mocked up at the moment but the designers want you to design a banner for the website in Adobe Illustrator. The Aim:

The aim would be to get people to come and visit Blossom in the park, this is not just your ordinary flower show, its a show for all the family. It’s a family day out, of fun and education you would be encouraged to bring your own old objects, junk, stuff that you might have thrown away, you would be encouraged to recycle and upcycle these objects and make them into something different and place them into a garden so that visitors could engage with them. There would be different types of gardens on display; your normal cottage garden, a city garden, biodiversity/sustainability gardens, along with wild flower gardens. Lots to see and do, and when you are tired and hungry hang out in some of the cool log cabins/tree houses to grab a bite to eat and refuel with some gourmet food and coffee. Lots for the children to do, including little hiding play areas with wild flower gardens.

Usage:

My brand idea is made up of many different parts, just like the show its full of different parts to explore and engage with. I have used a few different typefaces here something I would not normally do but here I think it works. Along with this I have used four different colours, giving it a sense of playful fun for all ages. Around the letterforms are little icons that give a sense to the target audience what you might expect to see and do at the show. The branding would appear on festival flags, Tshirts, merchandise, colour systems, signage, maps, brochures etc.

Target Audience:

The target audience would be anyone how has a sense of fun, families with children looking for a super fun day out in the hot summer sun. You will learn from this and hopefully take something away that you can adapt to use in your own home and garden in terms of recycling, upcycling and biodiversity/sustainability. Anyone with an interest in gardening and to encourage others how might not be so greened figured. Not just your run of the mill flower show!

Vantastival App

My festival of choice is Vantastival, an independent family run festival that has been held in Co.Louth since May 2010. Its described on the website as “ a crazy idea that grew legs and, with the help of brothers, sisters, parents, friends and many complete strangers, we pulled off the first Vantastival on a wing and a prayer in May 2010”.

It’s a festival that incorporates peoples love for the outdoors, music, campervans and camping. It appealed to me as it is a unique event, it brings together many different types of people; some have a love of campervans, others a love of music but all with a desire to sit back and enjoy life, forget about the hustle and bustle of it for a weekend, kick back enjoy the music, chill out with people from different walks of life who share their passions.

The whole festival has a very relaxed vibe around it so I wanted that to be reflected in the design of the website and app. The location is key to the marketing of the festival, it adds to the whole chilled out vibe so I knew that I wanted to incorporate the surrounding mountains into the design, it’s a festival that showcases Irish musicians and promotes itself as being a family run event that feels like the festival goer is part of the family so I wanted the the site and app to be comfortable for the user. The forest was also a key part of the design I wanted to include them into the footer of the app and in the website itself.

The logo design at its core uses the VW camper van, the title of the festival arches over the whole design in a slab san serif typeface Rockwell bold. The use of floral and leaf designs spiral out from the strong circle the surrounds the VW camper van icon. This gives a more hippy vibe like flower power of the 60’s and 70’s it gives it a vintage feel, the colours I have chosen to use also harp back to a vintage time with the Retro Red, Cool Mint and Cool Green all colours that you would see on VW camper vans of the 60’s and 70’s.

Overall, I feel all elements of my design reflect the ethos of the festival very well.

Responsive Web Design - DSA - Project - HTML5, CSS3, Media Queries

My Profile Page - 2 COLUMN LAYOUT

Create a skeleton website with three pages and two layouts (A and B shown below). The first page will contain a dummy login form (in section A.2). Your form should have a field‐set and a legend, as well as the following three fields:

  • A username field (type="text")
  • A password field (type="password") A login button (type="submit")

You should write CSS selectors for each of these in order to style them uniquely. Include your CSS in a separate file and link each of your pages to it.

Page two should be a profile page. Imagine that a user has successfully logged into your site, so welcome them in some way. You can hardcode the details on this page ‐ it doesn't need to know anything about the login form details at this stage. This page should contain the following elements.

A profile picture (yours, or some dummy content ‐ in section B.2) Some user information (e.g. last login, tagline, or similar ‐ in section B.2) A text block with some dummy content (in section B.3)

Page three must contain a 300 word discussion of you design rational, choice of colour scheme, etc. (in section A.2). Feel free to do more than 300 words if you wish.

Section A.1 and B.1 will contain a Navigation Bar with links to the three pages. This is a bit redundant for the first page, since in the real world your login form would forward the user onto the main page ‐ but your form will not be functional and we will still need a way to navigate at this stage!
